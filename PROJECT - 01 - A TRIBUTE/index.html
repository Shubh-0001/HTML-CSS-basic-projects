A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile man of india

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India. Known as the "Missile Man of India," he played a key role in the development of India's missile and space programs. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings through hard work, dedication, and a passion for learning. Dr. Kalam inspired millions of young people with his vision for a developed India and his belief that dreams can be transformed into reality through knowledge and perseverance. His contributions to science, education, and nation-building continue to inspire people around the world.

biography

  • Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • Indian aerospace scientist and statesman.
  • Known as the "Missile Man of India".
  • Played a major role in India's missile and space programs.
  • Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
  • Inspired millions of students through his speeches and books.
  • Received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
  • Passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture in Shillong.
  • Remembered for his humility, vision, and dedication to the nation.
